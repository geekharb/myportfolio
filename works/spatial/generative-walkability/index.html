Generative Walkability

A metric to understand urban environment.

Project Synopsis

The project explores using generative process as a tool to understand and improve the complicated urban environment. By mapping the existing urban elements in the site, we devised a performance metric to measure the quality of a building, then a path. We then use the metric to assess all the paths from the MRT stations to each of the destinations within the zone. With such a metric, we are then able to computationally optimise the location and quantity of potential future developments such that these developments achieve the largest impact.

The Site

Our site consists of the entire One-North business park district located at Queenstown, Singapore. The 200-ha development was conceived by JTC Corporation as a research and business park that is operated by the knowledge economy. The site was chiefly master-planned to integrate industrial institutes with residential communities, educational institutes, social and recreational amenities into a singular work-live-play-learn environment.

We realised from site studies that street activity is low and is a far cry from the mixed used vision. Therefore, we decided to focus on improving the walking experience in the neighbourhood, through the addition of amenities, but where and how much to add?

Performance Metric

Various aspects which makes an urban space appealing to visitors are mapped and the results combined into an overall performance metric for each building. The diagrams break down each aspect, with an overall diagram visualizing the combined metric.

Destination

Four vironoi are generated with the 4 MRT stations within the study area as the center of each voronoi. All points within each voronoi would be closest to its center. Thus we make an assumption here that if one has destination at any building in the area, he/she would alight at the MRT station in the voronoi center, before travelling to the destination.

Shortest Path

Shortest paths to each of the destinations within the vironoi are generated. Virtual agents with a specific field of view are then released to identify buildings that one would visually encounter as they travel the paths to their destination. Points are then tabulated to obtain the performance of each shortest path.

Amenity Amount and Placement

Using a hypothetical 100 points worth of amenities, we sought to determine the optimal location and amount of amenities to add to the area. We identified 9 potential plots. Using evolutionary optimiser, we allowed the optimiser to freely place amenities on 4 of the 9 plots each time, we then ran the optimiser to determine the ideal placement and amount of amenities.
