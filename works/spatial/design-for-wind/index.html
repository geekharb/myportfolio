Design for Wind

An iterative design and analysis tool.

Project Synopsis

The project attempts to design for a good wind environment on both the neighbourhood scale and the building scale in the initial massing design stage. Through the use of readily available data, using GIS and generative design on Grasshopper, a quick workflow is devised, the result of which would be a starting point for more detailed design later.

Urban Scale Study

The study consists mainly of the larger Central Business District (CBD) in Singapore. The preliminary assessment on the district scale seeks to utilise the morphological method for a time-efficient analysis of the wind environment.

The morphological method looks at the ground coverage ratio (GCR) of a study grid, studies have shown a correlation between the wind speed and GCR (Ng, 2011), with a higher GCR correlating with lower wind speed. The GCR information is accessible to planners and thus provides a good enough determinant of the wind environment in the early stages of design.

Problem Identification

From the previous process, potential new air paths on the district and local level were identified and general mitigation strategies were proposed. Region 2 is then identified for building level design.

Zoning Strategy

CFD simulation is ran for the site with immediate surrounding, the result is then used to formulate the zoning strategy, taking advantage of existing wind environment.

Programmatic Distribution

The main programmatic distribution of the original buildings were retaining, resulting in 2 main iterations. The block of the left is then lifted 6m above ground to create a double colume space on the ground floor as per the zoning strategy.
