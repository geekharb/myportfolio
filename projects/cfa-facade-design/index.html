Melodies - CFA Facade Redesign

Project Sypnosis

The project stems from the a week-long competition for the concept design of NUS Center For the Arts (CFA) studio facade. The existing surrounding context for the side facade has experienced changes over the years such that the side facade has become the de facto front facade. The brief calls for a design which displays the CFA spirit of discovery, exchange and action.

The design draws inspiration from the angklung, a traditional musical instrument of Southeast Asia made usually with bamboo tubes. The instrument is often used to teach the basics of musical notes. The facade integrates the angklung patterns with real angklungs that allows passersby to learn and even exercise their creativity to create new melodies, while rejuvenating the front space. The parametric design process also allowed the logic to be applied to the nearby tunnel, in order to integrated the two spaces as an united whole.
